# Telco Customer Churn Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes customer churn using the Telco dataset, combining
business insights and financial impact evaluation.

## Key Questions
- What factors drive customer churn?
- Which customers are most at risk?
- What is the financial impact of churn?
- How can the business reduce churn?

## Data
- Source: IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset
- Unit of analysis: Customer
- Key variables: contract type, tenure, payment method, monthly charges

## Methodology
- Data cleaning and preparation
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Churn segmentation analysis
- Revenue impact estimation

## Outputs
- Business analysis notebook
- Financial analysis notebook
- Key insights and recommendations

## Repository Structure
data/        # Dataset  
notebooks/   # Analysis notebooks (business + financial)  

## Key Insights
- High churn among month-to-month customers  
- New customers are the most vulnerable  
- Certain payment methods increase churn risk  
- Churn represents a direct revenue loss  

## Conclusion
## Conclusion
The analysis shows that customer churn is both a behavioral and financial problem.

From a business perspective, churn is concentrated among month-to-month customers, electronic check users, and newer customers with short tenure. From a financial perspective, this is especially important because churned customers account for 30.5% of the monthly revenue analyzed and also have higher average monthly charges than active customers.

Overall, the results suggest that the company should prioritize retention efforts in the early customer lifecycle, encourage longer-term contracts, and promote more stable payment methods. Reducing churn in these high-risk segments would improve both customer retention and revenue stability.
